An out of town developer has <a href="http://hillsandheights.org/2012/06/20/springhill-residents-concerned-about-proposed-housing-development/">submitted plans for constructing low-income housing</a> on the vacant lots on the 600 block of Cowardin Ave. This impacts our neighborhood tremendously, and in response the neighborhood is holding a meeting on <strong>Wednesday, June 27 at 7:00 PM at 603 W. 19th St.</strong>

Springhill resident Jason has compiled a list of reasons why this development is undesirable:
<blockquote>Our small neighorhood already supports a 70 unit low income facility.  With less than 40 single family homes in the neighborhood, we are already taking our share of the low income burden in the city.  A google search shows a significant concentration of these types of apartment properties, exclusively on southside and northside; none turn up in the areas west of VCU.  My concern is that we have reached our "Threshold" and we risk overconcentration.  Which in <a href="http://stardust.asu.edu/research_resources/research_files/49/90/Download_the_Research_Brief_in_PDF_format.pdf">the study by arizona state university</a> suggests reasults in "stagneant or decling property values."

The city should focus it's development of low income homes/apartments in areas not already burdened by it.

Studies on crime and low-income housing done by Indiana University and Purdue University <a href="http://www.marinscope.com/articles/2010/11/03/novato_advance/opinion/doc4cd1a230c361a775871921.txt">state</a> that "rates of violent crime are generally higher in Areas with high-density Residential development."  I cannot recall the exact numbers, but neighborhood is actually one of the most densly populated areas in Richmond thanks to the 3 large apartment buildings.

We are historically an area of single family homes,  and are being overun by high density development.

I do not believe this particular devlopment provides zoning minimum, parking, setback, open area requirements, floor to area ratio's of building to buildable open space.  We already have a parking problem and are working with the city to solve it. (i was unable to park infront of my house lastnight.  It happens 1-2 times a week.)</blockquote>
